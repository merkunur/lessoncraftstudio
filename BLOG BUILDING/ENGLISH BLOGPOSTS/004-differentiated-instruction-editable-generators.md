## The Science: Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development

**Three learning zones**:

1. **Zone of Current Ability**: Student can complete independently (no learning, boredom)
2. **Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)**: Student can complete with scaffolding (**optimal learning**)
3. **Zone of Frustration**: Student cannot complete even with support (shutdown, anxiety)

**Differentiation goal**: Place each student in their ZPD

**Example** (spelling practice):
- **Core content**: Same 10 words for all students
- **Emma's ZPD**: Words + images + 50% letters revealed (Easy mode)
- **Marcus's ZPD**: Words + images + 25% letters revealed (Normal)
- **Sophia's ZPD**: Words only, 0% letters (No Clues mode)

**Research**: Students in appropriate ZPD retain 2.8√ó more vs one-size-fits-all (Tomlinson, 2001)
