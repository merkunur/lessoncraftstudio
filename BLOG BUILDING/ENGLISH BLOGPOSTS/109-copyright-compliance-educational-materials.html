Understanding Copyright Law

‚úÖ Public Domain (Free to Use)

‚Ä¢ Some Creative Commons content (released)

‚ùå Still Copyrighted (Requires Permission)

‚Ä¢ Most commercial worksheets
‚Ä¢ Contemporary images and photos
‚Ä¢ Licensed characters (Disney, etc.)

Fair Use in Education

Fair use is a limited exception to copyright, not a blanket permission for educational use. Courts evaluate fair use using four factors‚Äîall must be considered together.

The Four-Factor Test

Factor 1: Purpose and Character of Use

Favors fair use:
‚úì Educational, non-profit use
‚úì Classroom instruction
‚úì Teaching and learning

Against fair use:
‚úó Commercial purpose
‚úó For-profit tutoring
‚úó Selling materials

Factor 2: Nature of the Copyrighted Work

More likely fair use:
‚úì Factual works (textbooks, encyclopedias)
‚úì Published works

Less likely fair use:
‚úó Creative works (novels, poems, artwork)
‚úó Unpublished works

Factor 3: Amount and Substantiality Used

More likely fair use:
‚úì Small portion (one chapter, not whole book)
‚úì Only what's necessary for lesson

Less likely fair use:
‚úó Entire work used
‚úó "Heart" of the work

Factor 4: Effect on Market Value

Favors fair use:
‚úì Does NOT harm sales of original
‚úì Not replacing purchase

Against fair use:
‚úó Replaces need to buy original
‚úó Causes financial harm to creator

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Note

All 4 factors must be considered together. Fair use is not a simple checklist‚Äîit requires nuanced analysis for each specific situation.
