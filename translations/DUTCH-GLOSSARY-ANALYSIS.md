## üéØ DUTCH-SPECIFIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Compound Words
- Dutch loves compound words like German
- Write as one word without spaces: "Werkbladmaker" not "Werkblad maker"
- Use hyphens sparingly (only when needed for clarity)

### U-Form (Polite Address)
- Use polite u-form for instructions: "Selecteer..." not "Selecteer jij..."
- Professional software should always use formal address

### Loanwords
- Dutch freely adopts English tech terms (download, upload, website)
- But prefer Dutch terms when they're equally clear and common
- "Beeldbank" is better than "Afbeeldingsbibliotheek" even though both work

### Educational Context
- Prefer education-specific terms over technical terms
- "Opgave" (exercise) over "Probleem" (problem)
- "Uitkomst" (result) over technical terms in math

### Apostrophes in Plurals
- Dutch uses apostrophes for some plurals: "thema's" not "themas"
- This is grammatically correct in Dutch
