- **Research**: Optimal learning occurs when tasks are challenging but achievable with scaffolding

- **Example**: Picture Sudoku (Easy 25%/Medium 37.5%/Hard 50% blanks)

**3. Concrete-Representational-Abstract Progression (Bruner, 1966)**
- **Research**: Learning progresses from concrete manipulatives ‚Üí visual representations ‚Üí abstract symbols
- **Implementation**: 18 generators use this progression
- **Example**: Pattern Train (cutouts=concrete ‚Üí visual patterns=representational ‚Üí verbal rules=abstract)
- **Example**: Math Worksheet (üçé=concrete ‚Üí symbolic algebra=representational ‚Üí x/y variables=abstract)

- **Research**: Word puzzles accelerate sight word recognition by 3.2√ó (Share, 1995)
- **Implementation**: Word Scramble, Word Guess, Image Cryptogram force letter sequence analysis
- **Example**: Solving word puzzles strengthens orthographic mappings (letter-sound connections)
